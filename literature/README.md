# Literature references

The PDFs of the foundational papers for this project were kept locally during development but are not redistributed in this repository. The citations below identify every source used in `docs/lit_review.md` and throughout the library documentation.

1. Pevzner, P. A., Tang, H., and Waterman, M. S. (2001). An Eulerian path approach to DNA fragment assembly. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 98(17), 9748 to 9753. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.171285098

2. Zerbino, D. R. and Birney, E. (2008). Velvet: algorithms for de novo short read assembly using de Bruijn graphs. *Genome Research*, 18(5), 821 to 829. DOI: 10.1101/gr.074492.107

3. Compeau, P. E. C., Pevzner, P. A., and Tesler, G. (2011). How to apply de Bruijn graphs to genome assembly. *Nature Biotechnology*, 29(11), 987 to 991. DOI: 10.1038/nbt.2023

4. Idury, R. M. and Waterman, M. S. (1995). A new algorithm for DNA sequence assembly. *Journal of Computational Biology*, 2(2), 291 to 306. DOI: 10.1089/cmb.1995.2.291

5. Chikhi, R. and Medvedev, P. (2014). Informed and automated k-mer size selection for genome assembly. *Bioinformatics*, 30(1), 31 to 37. DOI: 10.1093/bioinformatics/btt310

6. Marcais, G. and Kingsford, C. (2011). A fast, lock free approach for efficient parallel counting of occurrences of k-mers (Jellyfish). *Bioinformatics*, 27(6), 764 to 770. DOI: 10.1093/bioinformatics/btr011

7. Bankevich, A., Nurk, S., Antipov, D., et al. (2012). SPAdes: a new genome assembly algorithm and its applications to single cell sequencing. *Journal of Computational Biology*, 19(5), 455 to 477. DOI: 10.1089/cmb.2012.0021

8. Cormode, G. and Muthukrishnan, S. (2005). An improved data stream summary: the count min sketch and its applications. *Journal of Algorithms*, 55(1), 58 to 75. DOI: 10.1016/j.jalgor.2003.12.001
